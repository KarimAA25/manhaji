# Manhaj Rubric Framework — proprietary IP

**Why this is ours, not the school's.** Schools score grades; nobody in K-12 scores *competencies* consistently across subjects. Our differentiator is a single rubric framework applied uniformly across every subject, term after term, so a parent report's strengths/weaknesses radar means the same thing in Math as it does in Art. The framework below is what we deliver — schools do **not** get to tweak the axes (they can tweak the descriptors).

## The six universal axes

Every subject, every grade, every term scored on the same six axes. This is what powers the radar chart in the parent report.

| Axis | What it measures | Example "strong" evidence | Example "weak" evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Analytical reasoning** | Decomposing problems, identifying patterns, drawing valid inferences | Solves multi-step problems unprompted; spots edge cases | Skips steps; can't explain *why* an answer works |
| **Creative expression** | Generating novel ideas, framing problems in new ways, original output | Proposes a different approach than taught; original project topics | Reproduces examples verbatim; safe choices only |
| **Oral communication** | Speaking with structure, listening actively, defending a position | Leads discussion; rephrases peers' points before responding | Quiet; answers in single words; avoids speaking |
| **Written expression** | Structure, clarity, grammar, register-appropriate language | Sustained argument over multiple paragraphs; controlled register | Run-ons; idea jumps; mechanical errors block meaning |
| **Class participation** | Showing up engaged: questions asked, peer interactions, focus | Asks clarifying questions; helps peers; on-task | Passive; off-task with phone; doesn't engage with material |
| **Homework consistency** | On-time submission, completeness, evidence of effort | All on time, complete; revises after feedback | Late or missing; partial completion; no engagement with feedback |

## Scoring scale

| Score | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Emerging | Below grade-level expectation; needs significant support |
| 2 | Approaching | Inconsistent; needs targeted intervention |
| 3 | Meeting | At grade-level expectation |
| 4 | Exceeding | Above grade-level; could mentor peers |
| 5 | Mastering | Demonstrates beyond-curriculum capability; ready for stretch |

Scores recorded as **decimals 1.0–5.0** so monthly drift is visible (e.g. 3.7 → 3.4 over two months is a flag even if "meeting" stays the same).

## Subject lenses (modify *descriptors only*, not axes)

The six axes are universal, but the *evidence* a teacher looks for differs by department. We ship preset descriptor packs. Schools can adjust the descriptor wording for their context; they cannot add/remove axes.

- **STEM lens** (Math, Sciences, ICT) — Analytical weight 1.5×; "creative" = problem-reframing or novel proof routes
- **Languages lens** (English, Arabic, French) — Written + Oral both weight 1.3×; "analytical" = textual analysis
- **Humanities lens** (History, SSE, SSA, Civics, Economics) — Written weight 1.3×; "analytical" = source critique, argument construction
- **Arts/PE lens** (Art, Music, PE) — Creative weight 1.5×; "homework" = practice-time-tracked; "analytical" = critique vocabulary
- **Religious/cultural lens** (Islamic Studies) — Standard weights; "creative" de-emphasised (text-based discipline)

## Composite scores derived from the six axes

These are computed; teachers don't enter them directly.

- **Term grade narrative weight**: 60% subject-specific assessments + 40% rubric composite
- **Strength signal**: top-2 axes consistently > 4.0 across all subjects → that becomes the student's "strongest dimension"
- **Watch signal**: any axis < 2.5 in 2 consecutive months → auto-flagged in parent report + teacher dashboard
- **University-fit signal (G9+ only)**: weighted rubric scores per subject × historical alumni-placement weights → tier probability bands. Computed monthly. **Always shown as historical bands, never as a prediction.**

## Cadence

| Action | Cadence | Who enters it |
|---|---|---|
| Per-rubric scoring of each student | Monthly (last week of each month) | Subject teacher |
| Adjustment after major assessment | Within 1 week of assessment | Subject teacher |
| Composite & university-fit refresh | Nightly (automatic) | Manhaj system |
| Rubric descriptor review with school | Once per academic year | Curriculum coordinator + Manhaj CSM |

## Why this framework will close the school's mind

- **Apples-to-apples across subjects** — a parent comparing Math and Art rubric scores is comparing the *same* axes, not a 65 in Math vs a 91 in Art. That's a story competitors can't tell.
- **Trend-visible** — monthly cadence with decimals means parents see *direction*, not just snapshots.
- **Defensible university-fit** — bands not predictions, anchored to the school's own alumni history.
- **Locked framework, flexible language** — schools get descriptor flexibility (Arabic phrasing, faith-context tuning) without breaking the cross-school data layer that lets us benchmark and improve the model.
